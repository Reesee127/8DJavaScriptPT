The 21st Century Skills

The 4C's of 21st century skills are:

  1. Critical Thinking
  2. Creativity
  3. Collaboration
  4. Communication

In this modern age wherein the development of technology is rapid, innovation is reshaping the way we live. We are in a digital age whrerein technology is thriving and continues to evolve. Thus, it is necessary to posess these 21st century skills to cope and embrace the challenges in this era. These skills are the key for modern students to succeed in both school and workplace in the future. This website tackles the 4C's of 21st century skills in depth.

The 4C's in depth:

Critical Thinking Creativity Collaboration Communication
Communication is the ability to effectively express oneself and understand others, using both verbal and non-verbal methods, and adapting communication styles for various situations and audiences. Critical thinking is the intellectually disciplined process of actively and skillfully conceptualizing, applying, analyzing, synthesizing, and/or evaluating information gathered from, or generated by, observation, experience, reflection, reasoning, or communication, as a guide to belief and action. This is important in the 21st Century because it is harder than ever to verify accurate information in this digital period. Creativity is the ability to generate original ideas, think outside the box, and find innovative solutions to problems, fostering adaptation and success in a rapidly changing world. It involves thinking in new ways, exploring different perspectives, and developing novel solutions. It is also the ability to combine existing knowledge and ideas in a unique way to create something new and valuable. Collaboration is the ability to work together to achieve a common goal, requiring individuals to pool their knowledge, skills, and efforts to achieve a shared outcome. It also signifies the ability to work effectively with others to achieve a common goal, encompassing skills like communication, problem-solving, and sharing ideas. Communication is the ability to effectively express oneself and understand others, using both verbal and non-verbal methods, and adapting communication styles for various situations and audiences.
